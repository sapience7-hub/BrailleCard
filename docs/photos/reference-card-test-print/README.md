## What this evidence does and does not establish

A photo of the cooled part confirms the front face printed, is legible at a
glance, and shows a connected heart outline, print text, and a Braille cell
in the expected relative positions. It does **not** substitute for:

- direct caliper measurement of dot base diameter, dot/cell/line spacing, or
  dot height against the ADA ranges in `docs/PRODUCT_GOAL.md`;
- a qualified Braille reviewer reading the physical dots by touch;
- tactile-tester evaluation of the heart silhouette;
- inspection of layer adhesion, warping, support-removal marks, or sharp
  edges, none of which are reliably visible in a photo.

These remain open items in `examples/sample-output/QUALITY_CONTROL.md` and
the "Legacy production-acceptance conditions" in `HANDOFF.md`; no checklist
item is marked complete on the basis of these photos alone.
